## Example (illustrative — Barron book MCQ assignment)

*Sample tied to “collect all Barron MCQ mistakes.” **Do not submit this text as your own**—write your real experience.*

### What changed compared to my plan

In `plan.md` I said I would log every missed Barron MCQ **the same day**. In practice, after **Practice Exam 1** I stopped after 10 rows and finished the rest the next morning, so two *Why I got it wrong* cells were vague. I also planned to go chapter-by-chapter, but I jumped to the **diagnostic** first; I still logged everything under **Barron**, but my *Source* labels mixed `Diagnostic Test A Q5` with `Ch 3 Review Q8` until I standardized the naming midweek.

### What was difficult

The **sheer number** of rows was tiring. The hardest MCQs were **inheritance and polymorphism** tracing: I often picked an answer before writing the compile-time type on paper. Filling *Correct rule / fix* forced me to state the rule in one sentence, which was slow but useful. Remembering whether a mistake was from **print book** or **Barron Online** for *Source* was annoying until I always prefixed online items with `Barron Online —`.

### What I learned

I learned that **one row per wrong question** in `mcq.md` makes review efficient: before the unit test I sorted mentally by *Reminder* and only redid topics that repeated (for example “check `ArrayList` remove + index shift”). I learned that **precise *Source*** (`Practice Exam 2 MCQ Q17`) saves time when I need to find the original explanation in Barron again.
